Green Sea Turtle

The green sea turtle, also known as the green turtle or Pacific green turtle, is named for the green color of its fat, rather than the color of its skin or shell as most people think.

  • Scientific name: Chelonia mydas
  • Conservation status: Endangered
  • Mass: 160 kg (Adult)
  • Size: 3-4 ft

The green sea turtle (Chelonia mydas) is a large sea turtle of the family Cheloniidae.These turtles' shells are in fact olive to black.Its range extends with two distinct populations in the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans, but also found in the Indian Oceans.They spend their lives submerged, but must breathe air for the oxygen needed to meet the demands of vigorous activity.
